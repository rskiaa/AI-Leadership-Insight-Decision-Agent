Northstar Dynamics Quarterly Report Q4 2025

Quarter Summary
Q4 revenue was $66 million, representing 14 percent year-over-year growth and 6 percent sequential growth. The strongest performance came from enterprise renewals, new cross-sell activity, and usage growth in AI-enabled modules.

Operational Metrics
Enterprise renewal rate reached 96 percent in the quarter.
Average contract value increased 11 percent due to multi-product adoption.
Implementation backlog rose by 18 percent, suggesting demand strength but also potential delivery pressure.
Support ticket resolution time improved by 22 percent after workflow automation and knowledge base improvements.

Segment Commentary
Logistics customers increased spend on planning and inventory optimization.
Retail customers remained cautious but showed interest in margin protection features.
Manufacturing customers delayed several expansion projects because of budget timing, though renewal risk stayed low.

Risks and Watch Items
Implementation staffing remains tight in Europe.
Delayed onboarding could affect revenue recognition in the next quarter.
Sales productivity for new hires is improving more slowly than expected.

Management Outlook
Management expects Q1 growth to remain healthy but uneven across segments. Priority areas are delivery capacity, time-to-value for new customers, and conversion of strong pipeline into implemented revenue.
